noun · Shetland

haaf

/hɑːf/

The deep sea beyond coastal waters; the deep-sea fishing carried out thirty to forty miles offshore, in open boats.

For centuries Shetland men rowed beyond the sight of land. They navigated by current and cloud, by the shift of the swell under a sixareen's keel, by a craft passed down without being written down.

Haaf is a quiet tribute to that inheritance — the patience, the instruments, the reading of invisible things. Watches made by a Shetlander, for anyone who keeps time by the sea.

Principles

Made the long way.

    Small batches. Every piece drawn, prototyped and tuned in Shetland before a single case is cut.

  • Capable

  • Honest

    Swiss automatic movements, solid steel, no shortcuts, no marketing theatre. A watch you can hand on.
